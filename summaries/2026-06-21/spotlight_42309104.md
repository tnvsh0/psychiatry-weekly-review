### Randomised controlled trials of psychological interventions treating common mental disorders in adults of Chinese descent: a systematic review of trial quality and credibility
**כתב עת:** ⭐ Lancet Psychiatry *(IF: 64.3)* | **סוג מחקר:** סקירה שיטתית | **מחברים:** Li S et al. | **תאריך:** 2026 Jul

BACKGROUND: Since 2000, there has been an increase in the number of randomised controlled trials of psychological interventions for common mental disorders in people of Chinese descent, which could provide important evidence regarding the cross-cultural effectiveness of psychological interventions. Systematic reviews of randomised controlled trials of psychological interventions, predominantly carried out in North America and Europe, have consistently identified methodological shortcomings; the quality of the evidence for people of Chinese descent has not been comprehensively evaluated. This systematic review aims to characterise the methodology and credibility of randomised controlled trials of psychological interventions for common mental disorders in people of Chinese descent (ie, ethnic Chinese populations).

METHODS: In this systematic review, we searched MEDLINE, Embase, PsycINFO, CNKI, and WANFANG to identify eligible randomised controlled trials published in English or Chinese from database inception to Sept 1, 2025. We included trials of psychological interventions in adults of Chinese descent (with at least 80% of Han Chinese descent) aged 18 years and older with a diagnosis of a common mental disorder. 12 reviewers independently and in duplicate judged selection against inclusion criteria and extracted data on study characteristics, aggregated outcome data, and journal characteristics. Trial design, conduct, reporting, and outcomes of included studies were evaluated using a structured quality framework. We calculated post-intervention standardised mean differences between groups to characterise the distribution of reported effects. We involved people with related lived experience in the research and writing process. This study is registered with PROSPERO, CRD42023394956.
