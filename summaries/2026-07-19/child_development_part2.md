### Constructive and destructive interparental conflict, parenting entropy, and child ADHD symptoms
**כתב עת:** 🔷 Development and Psychopathology (Dev Psychopathol) *(IF: 5.8)* | **סוג מחקר:** מאמר מחקרי | **מחברים:** Li Z et al. | **תאריך:** 2026 Jul 13

This multi-method, multi-informant, three-wave longitudinal study sought to examine how maternal and paternal parenting entropy might operate as indirect factors in the link between interparental conflict and the development of child attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) symptoms during early childhood. Participants were 235 families with a young child (Wave one:  M child age  = 2.97 years,  N girls  = 130, Child race/ethnicity: 56.2% White, 21.3% African American, 16.2% Mixed race) followed over three annual waves. We observed constructive and destructive interparental conflict during an interparental conflict discussion task and created parenting entropy based on multiple aspects of molar-rated parenting behavior during parent-child interaction. Child ADHD symptoms were reported by both parents over time. Findings indicated that only greater maternal parenting entropy operated as the indirect factor linking interparental conflict with elevated risks for child ADHD symptoms over time (Association with Maternal Parenting Entropy: Constructive conflict:  β  = -0.23, Destructive conflict:  β  = 0.17; Maternal parenting entropy in association with Child ADHD symptoms:  β s range: [0.16, 0.17]). Follow-up tests comparing the role of constructive vs. destructive forms of interparental conflict highlighted the role of constructive interparental conflict. Findings highlighted potential targets to mitigate the risks associated with unpredictable parenting behavior.

### Autonomic nervous system correlates of past and prospective suicidal thoughts and behaviors in Mexican-origin youth
**כתב עת:** 🔷 Development and Psychopathology (Dev Psychopathol) *(IF: 5.8)* | **סוג מחקר:** מאמר מחקרי | **מחברים:** Gonzalves LC et al. | **תאריך:** 2026 Jul 14

Few studies have examined prospective or neurobiological predictors of suicidal thoughts and behaviors (STB) in Latino youth. We hypothesized that autonomic nervous system (ANS) activity at baseline and in response to social exclusion would distinguish Mexican-origin adolescents with versus without histories of suicidality, and would predict subsequent suicidality. Skin conductance responses and respiratory sinus arrhythmia, indicators of sympathetic and parasympathetic activity, respectively, were recorded at baseline and during Cyberball exclusion trials, in 229 Mexican-origin youths ( M  = 17.16 years, 110 females). STB was assessed 11 times across ages 10 to 20 years; 62 youths had histories of STB prior to the physiological measures, and 61 youths reported STB in the following 2.5 years. Cyberball elicited parasympathetic withdrawal without sympathetic activation, compared to baseline. Adolescents with histories of STB, compared to those without, had lower sympathetic activity during baseline and social exclusion. Lower basal sympathetic activity paired with greater uncoupled sympathetic activation during Cyberball predicted prospective STB. These findings support bioecological theories of stress vulnerability that suggest acute autonomic activity may serve as a biomarker for suicidality risk and extend prior work by identifying pathophysiology that could be the target of interventions to reduce risk in Mexican-origin youth.

### Interaction between oral language comprehension and executive functions in typical development: A graph-theory analysis of behavioral data
**כתב עת:** 🔷 Child Development (Child Dev) *(IF: 5.4)* | **סוג מחקר:** מאמר מחקרי | **מחברים:** Faure L et al. | **תאריך:** 2026 Jul 13

This study investigates the evolving relationship between oral language comprehension (phonology, vocabulary, syntax) and verbal and nonverbal executive functions -EF- (inhibition, working memory, updating) in 395 typically developing French children aged 5 to 8 years (M = 7 years; SD = 11.91 months; 52% girls). Behavioral performances were analyzed using a graph-theory approach, allowing for the modeling of cognitive networks. Results showed a shift from early integration between language and EF in Kindergarten to increasing segregation and specialization by 3rd grade, associated with improvements in task performance and reduced interindividual variability along development. These findings highlight the dynamic between language comprehension and EF in children from the general population with further implications for developmental and clinical perspectives on language-related disorders.

This study examined the development of language understanding (including sounds, words and sentence structure) and executive functions (such as working memory and inhibition) in French children aged 5 to 8. Three hundred ninety-five children took part in the study. Using statistical models to map the connections between these skills through school-related development, the results show that language and executive functions are closely linked in Kindergarten. As children get older, however, these abilities begin to diverge and become more specialized. This change is linked to children performing better overall and becoming more similar in their abilities. These findings demonstrate that language and executive abilities in children are not fixed, but evolve as they grow and develop. This knowledge may help parents, teachers and professionals to provide better support for children experiencing language or learning difficulties.

### Care-based power-assertive parenting in rural Chinese families: Predicting children's adjustment outcomes
**כתב עת:** 🔷 Developmental Psychology (Dev Psychol) *(IF: 5.0)* | **סוג מחקר:** מאמר מחקרי | **מחברים:** Chen X et al. | **תאריך:** 2026 Jul 13

Among various notions about the distinguishing features of parenting in Chinese and some other East Asian societies, the care-based power-assertive parenting (CBPAP) perspective explicitly and deliberately emphasizes two integrated aspects of parenting, care and power assertion, and its role in helping children develop socially appropriate behaviors and achieve success in society. This 1-year longitudinal study examined how maternal CBPAP and paternal CBPAP contributed to children's adjustment in psychological, social-behavioral, and academic domains. Participants included elementary school students ( N  = 352; 193 boys), initially in fourth grade ( M age  = 10 years), in a rural region in China. Data were obtained from multiple sources including self-reports, teacher ratings, and school records. The results showed that, in general, parental CBPAP significantly and positively predicted later self-worth and social competence and negatively predicted later internalizing behaviors. The results indicate that parental CBPAP serves a constructive function in child development in a rural Chinese context. (PsycInfo Database Record (c) 2026 APA, all rights reserved).

### Adverse Childhood Experiences and Incarceration Among Men in the United States
**כתב עת:** 📄 Acad Pediatr | **סוג מחקר:** מאמר מחקרי | **מחברים:** Jackson DB, Testa A | **תאריך:** 2026 Jul 16

OBJECTIVES: To examine the association between adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) and prior incarceration and its features in a contemporary, nationally representative sample of US men.

METHODS: Data come from the 2022-2023 National Survey on Family Growth (NSFG), a cross-sectional survey of reproductive health, fertility, and family formation among a nationally representative sample of non-institutionalized, male and female household residents in the US (ages 15-49). The present study focuses on the adult male subsample, aged 18-49.

RESULTS: Findings indicate that ACEs are associated with prior incarceration in this sample of men, especially as ACEs accumulate (i.e., the adjusted predicted probability of incarceration for men reporting 5 or more ACEs is 0.23, compared to 0.05 for men reporting zero ACEs). While these patterns generally held across incarceration features, high ACEs exposure was particularly relevant for incarcerations of longer (v. shorter) durations (e.g., a year or more), and some of the largest point estimates occurred in the case of multiple (v. single) incarcerations and especially when examining 5 or more ACEs and recent incarcerations (i.e., past 12 months).

CONCLUSIONS: The impact of ACEs for formal criminal legal system contact appear to reverberate into adulthood and extend to incarcerations with particularly disadvantageous features (e.g., frequent, longer duration experiences). Considering the well-established implications of both ACEs and incarceration for myriad life domains - from health to education and employment to family life - the findings highlight the importance of early prevention and intervention efforts that disrupt pathways from early adversity to involvement in the criminal legal system.
