### Peer victimization and adjustment: Moderation by perceptions of same-race and other-race classmates' behavior
**כתב עת:** 🔷 Development and Psychopathology (Dev Psychopathol) *(IF: 5.8)* | **סוג מחקר:** מאמר מחקרי | **מחברים:** Troop-Gordon W et al. | **תאריך:** 2026 Jul 17

Drawing on the "social misfit" hypothesis, this study tested whether peer victimization is more predictive of maladjustment when children have many same-race classmates who they view as low in aggression. Also accounted for were children's race, the racial composition of the classroom, and children's perceptions of other-race peers. Data were collected in the fall, winter, and spring of a school year from 1,424 4 th  and 5 th  grade children ( M age  = 10.06;  SD age  = 0.67; Range age  = 8.43-13.03; 683 girls; 741 boys; 39.61% Black; 60.39% White) in the Southeastern United States. Measures included children's ratings of the aggressiveness of their participating Black and White classmates; teacher-reports of depressive symptoms, anxiety, and social withdrawal; and peer-reports of peer victimization, aggression, and prosocial behavior. Consistent with the social misfit hypothesis, when children viewed same-race classmates as low in aggression, peer victimization was more strongly associated with internalizing problems in the fall for Black children ( R 2 : 0.05-0.16) and with heightened anxiety in the spring for White children with many same-race classmates ( R 2  = 0.09). Although some protective effects of viewing other-race classmates as aggressive emerged, associations with heightened anxiety ( R 2 : 0.06-0.09) underscored the need to promote positive interracial relationships to optimize children's mental health.

### Family context, directed and random exploration profiles, and child socioemotional functioning
**כתב עת:** 🔷 Development and Psychopathology (Dev Psychopathol) *(IF: 5.8)* | **סוג מחקר:** מאמר מחקרי | **מחברים:** Li Z et al. | **תאריך:** 2026 Jul 17

Guided by the evolutionary developmental perspective, this multi-method, longitudinal study seeks to examine "hyperparameters" that organize young children's learning. In particular, we adopt computational modeling and a person-centered approach to characterize children's exploration-exploitation profiles during a reward probability task. We also explored the family socioeconomic precursors and child psychological sequelae of the profiles. Participants were 243 families with a preschooler (Mean age = 4.60 years, 56% girls) from diverse racial and ethnic backgrounds (46% Black, 19% Latinx). Family socioeconomic risks were measured at wave one, and exploration-exploitation and socioemotional functioning were assessed two years later via a reward probability task and teacher report, respectively. Person-centered approach revealed three exploration-exploitation profiles, reflecting (a) a high-random exploration, (b) a high-directed exploration, and (c) a balanced exploration-exploitation profile. Greater family socioeconomic risk was linked to an elevated likelihood of membership in the high-random exploration profile (e.g., Z = -2.37). In turn, the high-random exploration profile exhibited the highest attention problems and greater externalizing problems. Findings have critical implications for understanding the processes through which children adapt to adverse family contexts.

### Childhood experiences partly explain adult outcomes after very preterm or very low birth weight
**כתב עת:** 🔷 Child Development (Child Dev) *(IF: 5.4)* | **סוג מחקר:** מאמר מחקרי | **מחברים:** Wolke D et al. | **תאריך:** 2026 Jul 14

This study assessed whether associations between very preterm birth (<32 weeks' gestation) or very low birth weight (<1,500 g; VPT/VLBW) and adult outcomes of physical and mental health, wealth, and social relationships are mediated by childhood experiences of neurodevelopmental impairment, academic achievement, mental health, and parent-child and peer relationships. A Bavarian population-based cohort (N = 489; VPT/VLBW = 260; term-controls (TC) = 229; 49.9% females, White) were assessed at birth, 6, 8, 13, and 26 years (1985-2014). VPT/VLBW was associated with adverse childhood experiences and adulthood outcomes. Disparities in academic achievement, parent-child relationship and impairment explained lower wealth and social relationships, lower mental health and adult-parent relationship, and lower physical health for VPT/VLBW versus TC adults. Targeting schooling and parenting may improve long-term outcomes for VPT/VLBW individuals.

Very preterm birth or very low birth weight (VPT/VLBW; <32 weeks gestation or < 1,500 g) compared to at-term birth (>37 weeks gestation) increases the risk of adverse outcomes in physical and mental health, wealth, and social relationships in adulthood. This study examines whether these adult outcomes can be explained by childhood experiences following VPT/VLBW. We find not only that VPT/VLBW compared to term-born participants had lower childhood and adult outcomes, but also that the lower childhood experiences were associated with lower adulthood outcomes. In particular, childhood academic achievement and child–parent relationship showed the strongest effects, followed by neurodevelopmental impairment. Thus, parenting and schooling may be primary targets for interventions in childhood to improve the long-term outcomes after VPT/VLBW birth.

### Within- and between-person associations between 24-hr movement behaviors and irritability in early childhood
**כתב עת:** 🔷 Developmental Psychology (Dev Psychol) *(IF: 5.0)* | **סוג מחקר:** מאמר מחקרי | **מחברים:** Bourke M, Phillips SM | **תאריך:** 2026 Jul 13

This study examined associations between 24-hr movement behaviors and irritability in young children. Parents of 1,266 children (52% boys) from the Longitudinal Study of Australian Children completed 24-hr time-use diaries at ages 2 and 4, reporting sleep, physical activity, screen time, nonscreen sedentary behavior, and irritability. Bayesian multilevel compositional data analysis and isotemporal substitution models assessed within- and between-person effects. Children who typically spent more time in physical activity were irritable significantly less often, while more screen time was linked to greater time being irritable. On days when children were more active than usual, irritability was less common. Substituting screen or sedentary time with physical activity was associated with reduced irritability, suggesting that promoting physical activity may support early childhood mental health. ### Adverse childhood experiences and body appreciation: The buffering role of posttraumatic growth
**כתב עת:** 📄 Body Image | **סוג מחקר:** מאמר מחקרי | **מחברים:** Wang S et al. | **תאריך:** 2026 Jul 14

Adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) are widely recognized as risk factors for long-term psychological difficulties, including body dissatisfaction. However, the link between ACEs and positive body image, especially body appreciation, remains underexplored worldwide. Posttraumatic growth (PTG) may buffer the impact of ACEs, yet its role with ACEs has rarely been examined. This study investigated the association between ACEs and body appreciation and explored whether PTG moderated this association in a sample of Chinese adult men and women. The study included an online sample of 230 Chinese men and 251 Chinese women who had experienced one or more ACEs. Participants completed validated self-report measures of ACEs, PTG, and body appreciation. Pearson correlation and moderation analyses were performed separately by gender and by ACE subtypes. Results showed that higher levels of ACEs were generally associated with lower body appreciation in men and women, with stronger effects in women. Higher PTG weakened the negative association between ACEs and body appreciation among women, but not men. Subtype analyses indicated that PTG exerted buffering effects across multiple ACE domains among women, even after correction for multiple comparisons, whereas in men, subtype-level interaction effects were not significant. These findings indicate that PTG may act as a buffer of the association between childhood adversity and positive body image, particularly among women. While continued, namely longitudinal, research is needed, findings provide preliminary support for PTG-enhancing strategies to promote women's body appreciation, whereas interventions for men may need to target specific adversities.
